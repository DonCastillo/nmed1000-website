I am passionate about anything. Sometimes people tell me that I am nerd and I could not argue with that. My curiosity about the world and how they work has helped me become more understandable, sympathetic, and imaginative. Below are some of my many passions that have helped me become a better person:

They say that life imitates art, and art reflects life. Movies, TV, books, or any kind of literature help someone temporarily escape from reality and subject himself/herself to the other world. This is why I am so passionate about literature and films. The stressful condition of reality is somewhat eliminated once I open my Netflix account or turn a page of a book.

Films

I like watching movies that tackle social issues the world has experienced and will experience. The movie genres I usually watch are historical movies especially about World War II and the Holocaust. I am curious to know how people were dealing with this kind of distressful and conflicted environment. Some of my personal favorites are Schindler's List (1993), The Pianist (2002), Grave of the Fireflies (1988), and Saving Private Ryan (1998).

I also like watching true-to-life stories about people surviving from a life-threatening situation. One of my favorites is Alive (1993), which tells the story of a rugby team who got stranded in the middle of the snowy Andes Mountains after their plane crashed. Another survival movies I like are Rescue Dawn (2006) and Lone Survivor (2013). Another heart-wrenching movie worth mentioning here, although the protagonist did not survive in the story, is the movie Into the Wild (2007) which tells the story of Christopher McCandless who chose to liberate himself and lived his remaining years in the wild before he died due to starvation. The movie made me read the book that the movie was based on and watch documentaries about his past life on YouTube.

Another movie genre that I like watching are dystopian movies which depict societies where every aspect of human life is deprived and where people live in a chaotic and abusive situations. The common themes present in dystopian movies are about authoritarian governments, human rights abuses, environment degradation, apocalypse, and the loss of humanity. These movies are cautionary tales that I think should be watched in order to rethink the things we've taken for granted and prepare us on what lies in the future if we continue to ignore the freedoms we have now. Some of my personal favorite movies of this genre are Nineteen Eighty-Four (1984), which is based on the famous novel of the same name written by George Orwell, Children of Men (2006), The Hunger Games (2012-2015), Battle Royale (2000), V for Vendetta (2005), and The Matrix (1999).
